Sourdough Bread

This classic sourdough bread is made with a natural starter, resulting in a deep, tangy flavor and a perfectly chewy crust. With a long fermentation process, this bread develops complex flavors and a light, airy crumb.

500g (4 cups) bread flour

350g (1½ cups) water

10g (2 tsp) salt

100g (½ cup) active sourdough starter

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour and water until fully incorporated. Let it rest for 30 minutes (autolyse).
  2. Add the sourdough starter and salt, then mix until a sticky dough forms.
  3. Cover and let the dough rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Perform a series of four stretch-and-folds every 30 minutes over 2 hours.
  5. Cover and allow the dough to bulk ferment at room temperature for 4-6 hours until doubled in size.
  6. Lightly flour a surface, shape the dough into a round ball, and place it into a floured banneton (proofing basket) or bowl.
  7. Cover and refrigerate overnight for cold fermentation.
  8. Preheat the oven to 475°F (245°C) with a Dutch oven inside.
  9. Carefully place the dough onto parchment paper, score the top with a blade, and transfer it into the hot Dutch oven.
  10. Bake covered for 20 minutes, then uncover and bake for another 20-25 minutes until golden brown.
  11. Let the bread cool on a wire rack before slicing.
